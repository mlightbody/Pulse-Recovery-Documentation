# Sample Advice Output

## Example Session

This sample shows how Pulse Recovery might interpret a short, relatively intense workout such as walking or running up and down stairs.

The session produced a high heart-rate response, followed by a steady recovery over the next two minutes.

## Headline Recommendation

**Maintain**

---

## Session Advice

Your heart-rate recovery response was encouraging. Your heart rate dropped by 26 bpm in the first minute and by 40 bpm after two minutes, which suggests that your body began recovering well once the exercise stopped.

However, the session also appears to have created meaningful strain. Your perceived effort score was high, which suggests that the workout felt demanding even though the recovery numbers were reasonably strong.

For your next session, it may be better to maintain your current training intensity rather than increase the workload immediately. If similar sessions start to feel easier while your recovery remains stable or improves, gradual progression may become more appropriate.

---

## Trend Advice

Your recent recovery pattern appears to be slightly improving. Compared with your recent average, this session showed a somewhat stronger first-minute and two-minute recovery response.

That is a positive sign, especially if the workout type and intensity were broadly comparable with previous sessions. It may suggest that you are adapting to your current level of training.

At the same time, your high perceived effort score is worth noting. Recovery is not just about the heart-rate drop; how the session felt also matters. If high effort scores continue, it may be sensible to hold your current level for a little longer before increasing intensity.

---

## What Drove the Advice

The recommendation was influenced by several factors:

* **Good first-minute recovery**
  Heart rate dropped meaningfully during the first minute after exercise.

* **Continued recovery at two minutes**
  Heart rate continued to fall rather than flattening or rebounding.

* **High session strain**
  The perceived effort score suggests the session felt hard.

* **Stable post-exercise feeling**
  The user did not report feeling especially poor afterwards.

* **Slightly improving trend**
  Recent recovery values appear to be moving in a positive direction.

Together, these signals suggest that the user is recovering reasonably well, but that immediate progression may not be necessary. Maintaining current intensity while monitoring the next few sessions would be a sensible interpretation.

---

## Notes

This is an illustrative example rather than medical advice. Heart-rate recovery can be affected by many factors, including sleep, stress, hydration, heat, illness, caffeine, medication, workout type, and day-to-day fatigue.

Pulse Recovery is intended to support fitness and recovery reflection, not to diagnose or monitor medical conditions.
